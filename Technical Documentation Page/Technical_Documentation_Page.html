Introduction

This site is designed to inform about the types of dinosaurs on Isla Nublar

  • There are several ways in which we classify our dinosaurs.
    • Classification Guide

    There are two main types of dinosaurs on the island:

  • Carnivores (Meat Eaters)
  • Herbivores (Plant Eaters)

    • We will also show a few of the parks newest additions:

  • Hybrids
    • Carnivores

    These Predators eat meat and are the most dangerous to work with.

    The three most notable are:

  • T-rex (A strong, sharp toothed, and fast predator)
  • Spinosaurus (A massive creature with a long spine on its back)
  • Raptors (Fast, agile, and cunning predators that work together in teams)
    • Herbivores

    These creatures eat plants. Though they don't eat meat they are still very dangerous if one is not careful near them.

    The three most notable are:

  • Stegosaurus (An animal with that has plates on its back and spikes on its tail used for defence.)
  • Triceratops (a bull with a frilled head that has 3 massive horns on it)
  • Brachiosaurus (One of the tallest dinosaurs to roam the earth)
    • Hybrids

    Theorectically all dinosaurs are hybrids in the park, but those in this group are the ones that have been created and are entirely new.

    A hybrid's DNA is a collection of different creatures that have been spliced into one.

    The five most notable are:

  • Indomious Rex (Similar to the T-rex, but with more teeth and speed)
    • DNA: Tyrannosaurus rex, Velociraptor, Carnotaurus, Giganotosaurus, Majungasaurus, Rugops, and Therizinosaurus, as well as modern animals like cuttlefish, tree frogs, and a pit viper snake.
  • Indo Raptor (A newer version of indominous that is smaller and more useable in war than the Indominous Rex)
    • DNA: Tyrannosaurus rex, Velociraptor (A much bigger portion of the creature's DNA is based on this dinosaur), Carnotaurus, Giganotosaurus, Majungasaurus, Rugops, and Therizinosaurus, [something with opposable thumbs added as well], as well as the modern animal: tree frogs. [The cuttlefish and viper seem to be removed]
  • Paradeinonychus (an amphibious, high-speed hunter)
    • DNA: Parasaurolophus, Deinonychus, as well as the modern animal: tree frogs.
  • Velocirapteryx (bred for mobility and ferocity)
    • DNA: Velociraptor, Archaeopteryx, as well as the modern animal: tree frogs.
  • Amargospinus (a ferocious carnivore with the size of a Brachiosaurus)
    • DNA: Amargasaurus, Spinosaurus, as well as the modern animal: tree frogs.
